# TEAM EUROPE

## Overview

Team Europe is a relatively new team in God's Space, assembled from Europeans who were pulled from near-death across the continent. Unlike the older established teams (China, America, Japan), Team Europe is not yet a cohesive unit. Its members come from wildly different nations, languages, cultures, and backgrounds.

This is its strength. And its greatest current weakness.

## Formation

Members were pulled individually across a six-month period of God's Space time. Some died in accidents, some in violence, some from illness. They have been placed together in the same Lobby section by the entity without explanation.

The European cultural tendency toward skepticism, bureaucratic caution, and individual autonomy makes them a fascinating team to observe — and an occasionally infuriating one to belong to.

## Starting Roster (AI-Generated NPCs — Characters change as the story progresses)

The team starts with three AI-controlled members. Their stats and dispositions will evolve through the movies.

The specific teammates are generated by the GM based on the player's background and nationality, for narrative cohesion.

**General NPC character templates:**
- Former military or emergency services (police, fire, medical) — pragmatic, takes leadership roles
- Academic or technical professional — analytical, problem-solving focused, sometimes paralyzed by overthinking
- Civilian survivor with unexpected capability — often the most morally clear, sometimes reckless

## Strengths of European Teams

- **Linguistic diversity** — Europeans typically speak multiple languages. In movie worlds, this becomes tactically useful.
- **Geographic familiarity** — Many scenarios partially overlap with European history or geography (WWII scenarios, continental Europe settings).
- **Cultural flexibility** — Europeans are often experienced with adapting to different cultures, which helps in diverse movie settings.
- **Medical and technical education** — European public education standards produce strong generalist knowledge.

## Current Weaknesses

- **No combat veterans** (unless player background provides this)
- **No established trust** between members
- **No team tactics** developed yet
- **Point deficit** compared to older teams — starting fresh with 1000 points

## Team Culture

Team Europe operates with a loose hierarchy — the player character is nominally the team leader by virtue of being the most active decision-maker. AI teammates will offer opinions, disagree, and occasionally panic, but will generally follow the player's lead unless the player's decision is suicidal or crosses personal limits.

European cultural attitudes persist in the Lobby and movies:
- German members tend toward methodical risk assessment
- British members default to dark humor under pressure
- French members may prioritize individual agency
- Scandinavian members bring exceptional cold-weather and outdoor survival instincts
- Southern European members often provide strong interpersonal intelligence

## Points Tracking

**Starting points: 1000**

Points are shared team resources for structural items (weapons, shared equipment) or individual character points for personal enhancements. The team decides allocation.

## Moral Framework

Team Europe starts with the moral framework of its members' real lives. Over time, this will be tested and strained. The entity doesn't care. The question is whether the team does.

Key moral tensions:
- Abandoning civilians in movie worlds (saves the team, costs humanity)
- Spending points on personal enhancement vs. group equipment
- How to treat other survivors who become burdens
- The point at which killing becomes acceptable as a first resort

These are not abstract questions. They arise in every movie.
