# GOD'S SPACE — THE LOBBY

## Nature of the Space

God's Space — known internally by its coordinates but universally called "The Lobby" by those who survive long enough to name it — is a dimension maintained by an entity that calls itself nothing. Survivors refer to it as "God." It is not religious in origin. The entity simply exists, processes, evaluates.

The Lobby is physically a vast white hall of impossible scale. Corridors extend infinitely. Individual "rooms" manifest for each team. The architecture is sterile and featureless — polished white stone, no temperature, no wind, no scent except a faint antiseptic quality that many survivors describe as "the smell of somewhere that has never been alive."

Time in the Lobby does not correspond to time in the movie worlds. A week's rest in the Lobby may be minutes in the real world, or years. It does not matter.

## How People Arrive

God's Space takes people at the moment of death — or more precisely, at the moment of irreversible approaching death. A person who would die regardless within the next 60 seconds is pulled from their world.

They are given a choice: return to their death, or remain.

No one has ever chosen to return.

The entity presents this as mercy. Survivors are less certain.

## The Purpose

The entity has provided no direct explanation for the horror movie dimension travel. What is known:

- Participants are sent into real copies of events from various film and media narratives that exist across parallel world-lines.
- These events are not simulations. The zombies are real. The pain is real. The death is real.
- The entity observes all events but does not intervene.
- The entity rewards survival with points — a currency that can be exchanged for physical enhancements, weapons, and items within the Lobby's exchange system.

The operating theory among veteran survivors: it is a training program. For what, no one yet knows.

## The Exchange System

The Lobby contains exchange terminals — white kiosk-like structures where survivors can spend points. Items exchanged appear physically in the survivor's hands or equipment. Items cannot be taken out of God's Space without the entity's implicit permission — they arrive in the movie world pre-equipped.

Points are awarded for:
- Surviving the main objective of a movie (primary reward)
- Completing optional objectives (bonus rewards)
- Tactical excellence (GM discretionary bonus)
- Protecting other survivors (per-head bonus)

Points are deducted for:
- Deaths of allied team members (GM discretionary)
- Catastrophic failure of objectives

## The Team System

Survivors are organized into teams by rough geographic origin at the time of death. The entity does not explain this organization. The result:

- Team China
- Team America  
- Team Europe (current)
- Team Japan
- Team India
- [Other teams, unknown status]

Teams do not interact directly in movie worlds — each team has its own copy of the scenario. Teams can potentially encounter each other in the Lobby, though this is rare and not yet explained.

## Death and Restoration

When a participant dies in a movie world, their consciousness is returned to the Lobby. Their physical body is reconstituted from the point of last Lobby departure.

This means:
- Equipment lost in the movie world must be repurchased
- Damage done to the body during the movie does not persist (full healing on return)
- However: enhancements purchased and the genetic lock progression ARE maintained
- Memory of the movie continues — the trauma is real even if the body recovers

The entity appears to have no limit on how many times a participant can die and be restored. Whether this is unlimited is unknown.

## Ranking

The entity tracks what it calls "integration depth" — how fully a participant has optimized their physical and mental capabilities through the enhancement system. This is expressed in ranks:

**Unranked** → **F** → **E** → **D** → **C** → **B** → **A** → **S** → **SS** → **SSS**

Rank is not merely a number. Physical changes accompany progression:
- F rank: slightly improved baseline performance
- D rank: performance becomes genuinely superhuman in specific areas
- B rank and above: qualitatively different beings — speed, durability, and capability far beyond human norms

## Genetic Lock System

Each human body has genetic limitations hardwired by evolution. The entity has developed a method to shatter these limiters — called Genetic Lock Opening.

First Lock: Baseline enhancement, opens potential for further development. +5 all stats, +50 HP max.
Second Lock: Major enhancement. Specialized ability development. Requires first lock.
Third Lock and beyond: Approach superhuman capability. Require enormous point investment.

## The Unknown

Veteran survivors have noted:
1. The entity sometimes watches with unusual attention at key moments
2. Some enhancement options disappear from the exchange terminals after certain events
3. A small number of participants have been "retired" — removed from the program under unclear circumstances, presumably returned to their world or somewhere else
4. The movie scenarios sometimes diverge from their source material in ways that suggest the entity is testing specific things
5. Teams from different geographic origins appear to be on different point rates — Team Europe currently appears to receive slightly higher rewards per movie, possibly compensating for smaller starting numbers
