# Terror Infinity — Rival Teams & Endgame Structure

*Extracted from Volumes 5 and 10.*

---

## The Teams of God's Space

Every team operates in isolated copies of movie worlds. Teams are named by region (Team China, Team India, Team Europe, Team America, etc.). They do not normally share movie worlds — but under certain conditions, the System forces them to intersect.

**The ultimate goal:** Only **one team** will be able to leave God's Space at the end. All teams are eventually culled until one survives. The strong get stronger; the weak become stepping stones.

---

## Special Teams: Devil and Celestial

Beyond the regional teams, two special teams exist. The System does not normally explain their existence — characters learn about them through accumulated knowledge.

### Team Devil

- Composed of players with the **highest individual potential** across all teams
- Members are "cloned" into Team Devil by the System based on extraordinary performance — you can be copied without consent
- **No minimum rank requirement** — what matters is potential, not current power
- Growth rate is **faster than any other team** — their members evolve faster than normal players
- They will not negotiate. They kill other teams for points and do not warn first.
- Team Devil is considered **more dangerous than Team Celestial** despite Celestial's higher entry bar

> *"Team Devil is composed of players with the highest potentials... a normal person growing to such an extent within several movies. Their potential greatly surpassed ours."* — Vol 10

### Team Celestial

- Entry requirement: **second stage of genetic constraint unlocked** (E-rank minimum, Lock 2+)
- The "elite standard" team — high performers who met the bar
- A newly formed Team Celestial **beats** a newly formed Team Devil
- But Team Devil has higher growth ceiling — they overtake Celestial over time

> *"The basic requirement for entering team Celestial is the second stage of the unlocked mode. This is their advantage. A newly formed team Celestial will definitely win against a newly formed team Devil. However, team Devil has a higher rate of growth."* — Vol 10

## Inter-Team Combat Rules

When two teams are placed in the same movie world, the System's rules change:

- A mission notification announces the team encounter, usually mid-movie
- **Both teams' objectives shift** — now include killing the other team
- Only **one team can leave** — this is enforced by the System
- The movie's original objective may still need to be completed for the encounter to trigger or resolve

**Kill rewards (from Team Devil encounter notification, Vol 10):**
| Target | Points | Rank Reward |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Normal (unranked) member | +2,000 pts | Rank C |
| Unlocked member (F+) | +7,000 pts | Rank B |
| Own member killed | −1 point per kill |  |
| Own team kill of enemy | +1 point per kill | |
| Final score × 2,000 = bonus points |

**The System forces encounters.** It will adjust map size, timing, and mission parameters specifically to ensure the teams collide. Avoiding Team Devil is possible but requires the team to be smarter than the System's engineering. It is rarely a long-term option.

## Strategic Implications for Team Europe

- Team Europe is currently **Unranked** — below Team Celestial's entry bar, irrelevant to Team Devil for now
- Once 3+ members open their genetic constraint, the System **may begin placing Team Europe in shared movies** with other regional teams
- Team Devil will not appear until Team Europe is perceived as having genuine potential
- The question is not IF Team Europe encounters the special teams — it's **when, and whether they'll be ready**

> *"Honglu sneered. 'What kind of world do you think this is? Only one team will be able to leave. If we don't eliminate Team Devil today, they will grow at a much higher rate than us.'"* — Vol 10
